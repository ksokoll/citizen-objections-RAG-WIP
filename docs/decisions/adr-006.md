## ADR-006: Hallucination Strategy and Verification Routing

**Status:** Accepted

**Context:** The system generates Abwägungsstellungnahmen that cite specific legal paragraphs. A fabricated or incorrect paragraph citation in a public authority document is not a quality degradation but a legal and institutional failure. This is the primary risk of LLM-based generation in this domain.

**Decision:** The system applies a two-layer hallucination prevention strategy. Layer one is constrained prompting: the generation prompt explicitly prohibits the model from citing any paragraph not present in the provided retrieval context. If no relevant norm is found, the model is instructed to state the absence explicitly rather than interpolate. Layer two is post-hoc regex verification: every §-reference in the generated text is extracted, normalized to a canonical form, and matched against the `paragraph_id` fields of the retrieved chunks used for that generation. Any reference not found in the retrieved chunks sets `verified: False` on the corresponding `Rechtsgrundlage` entry.

Canonical normalization covers the following variant forms before matching: `§ 3 Abs. 1 BauGB`, `§3 (1) BauGB`, and `§ 3 I BauGB` all normalize to `baugb_§3_abs1`. Verification scope covers §-references only. Case law citations (BVerwG Aktenzeichen) and administrative regulations are explicitly out of scope for v1, with an ADR note that their inclusion requires a separate verified index.

If any `Rechtsgrundlage` in the generated output has `verified: False`, the `wuerdigungs_status` is set to `UNTERDRUECKT_UNVERIFIED` and `rechtliche_wuerdigung` and `abwaegungsergebnis` are set to `None`. The Sachbearbeiter receives Sachverhalt, Vorgebrachte Einwendung, Catalog-Match, and the list of Rechtsgrundlagen with their verified status, plus an explicit note requiring manual drafting of the legal assessment.

**Rationale:** Partial suppression of individual unverified sentences requires attributing prose sentences to specific citations at generation time, which requires structured per-argument generation and significantly increases prompt and output complexity. Hard failure on the entire Würdigung is simpler, more defensible, and consistent with the Hard Failure routing pattern established in prior projects. The Sachbearbeiter receives all necessary building blocks for manual drafting without the system producing a partially trustworthy output. In a public administration context, a partially shown draft that a Sachbearbeiter rubber-stamps is more dangerous than no draft at all.

**Rejected Alternatives:** Consultable failure with red-marked unverified citations shown to Sachbearbeiter. Rejected because rubber-stamping of flagged content is the documented failure pattern in human-in-the-loop systems under time pressure. Sentence-level suppression. Rejected due to implementation complexity without proportionate benefit.

**Consequences:** A normalization utility is required that maps citation variants to canonical `paragraph_id` format. This utility must be independently tested with known variant forms. The verification step runs after generation and before the result is written to the Audit Trail. `WuerdigungsStatus` is an enum with values `GENERIERT`, `UNTERDRUECKT_UNVERIFIED`, and `NO_MATCH`.
