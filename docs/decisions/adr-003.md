## ADR-003: Hybrid Retrieval with Reciprocal Rank Fusion

**Status:** Accepted

**Context:** The retrieval backend must find relevant legal norms from a corpus of structured law texts. Legal texts present two distinct retrieval challenges simultaneously: exact term matching (paragraph identifiers such as §3 Abs. 1 BauGB, specific legal terminology) and semantic similarity (arguments that paraphrase a norm without citing it directly).

**Decision:** The system uses hybrid retrieval combining BM25 sparse retrieval with FAISS dense retrieval. Result lists from both retrievers are merged using Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF). No cross-encoder reranker is used in v1.

**Rationale:** Dense retrieval alone fails on exact legal identifiers because embedding models treat paragraph numbers as low-signal tokens. Sparse retrieval alone fails on semantic paraphrase because it requires exact term overlap. RRF is the correct fusion mechanism because it operates on rank positions rather than raw scores, which removes the incompatible scale problem between BM25 and FAISS cosine similarity. RRF implementation is approximately six lines of Python with no additional dependencies. Excluding a cross-encoder reranker is a deliberate scope decision: the marginal retrieval quality gain does not justify the added latency and infrastructure complexity within the project timeline.

**Rejected Alternatives:** Dense-only retrieval (FAISS). Rejected because legal paragraph identifiers are not reliably retrieved by semantic similarity alone. Cross-encoder reranking over Top-N. Deferred to v2: adds latency and complexity without being the primary learning objective of this project.

**Consequences:** The retrieval layer has two separate retriever components and one fusion step. RRF parameters (k constant) are part of `retrieval_config_hash` for reproducibility. Both retrievers must be independently testable.
